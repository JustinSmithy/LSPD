Common Questions

How do I apply to a division?

Use the Apply page and select Traffic, FTO, or SWAT. Vice Unit applications are handled separately at vice-unit.com.

How long does approval take?

New officer accounts and applications are reviewed by department admins — timing depends on admin availability.

Can the public see arrest and seizure logs?

Yes — recent activity is shown publicly on the homepage. Full detail views require an approved officer login.

I logged in but can't log an arrest yet — why?

New officer accounts start as "pending" and need admin approval before they can create records.

Mission Statement. The Los Santos Police Department is committed to enhancing public safety and reducing crime through the delivery of exceptional police services. Priority classifications: Priority 1 life-threatening emergency, officer in danger, armed suspect · Priority 2 serious incident, property crime in progress, injury accident · Priority 3 routine call, minor violations, admin tasks.

Proper radio procedure: identify with unit number; clear, concise language; wait for acknowledgment; use phonetic alphabet; avoid chatter; emergency traffic takes priority; end with unit number.
Radio discipline: never use profanity; keep transmissions brief; speak slowly; no eating/drinking while transmitting; test equipment at shift start; report malfunctions immediately.

2.4 — RTO Standards (Radio Transmission Operations)

The Watch Commander or highest-ranking officer on duty sets the RTO level based on current operations.

LevelMode & Rules
PassiveStandard operations. Casual conversation permitted between calls; must maintain radio silence during active updates; harassment strictly prohibited. Default mode during routine patrol.
MidElevated operations. Limited non-essential comms; no casual conversation; strict silence during updates. Activated during multi-unit ops or elevated threat levels.
StrictCritical operations. Zero tolerance for non-essential comms; complete radio silence except updates; backup responses via text only. Reserved for critical incidents, SWAT ops, or department-wide emergencies.

Failure to comply with RTO standards results in disciplinary action ranging from verbal warning to suspension.

Section 4 · Daily Operations

4.1 — Standard Patrolling

Standard units operate marked vehicles throughout Los Santos and San Andreas, maintaining high visibility and proactive enforcement. Duties: traffic enforcement, 911 response, crime prevention, investigation of suspicious activity, arrest of wanted suspects, community engagement.

Two-officer patrol. Officers are strongly encouraged to patrol with a partner. Driver: vehicle control, navigation, radio comms, primary pursuit. Passenger: foot pursuit, suspect contact, equipment, backup coordination.

4.2 — Traffic Stops

PhaseActionsSafety
1. InitiationActivate lights; megaphone "pull over to the right"; position safely behind suspect.Safe following distance; well-lit area; notify dispatch.
2. PreparationOrder engine off; request backup; run registration.Observe behavior; check for weapons; assess threat.
3. ContactApproach driver side; professional greeting; explain stop; request documents.Tactical positioning; keep hands visible; watch for movements.
4. VerificationReturn to patrol vehicle; run MDC warrant checks; verify ownership.Maintain visual; monitor occupants; prepare for flight.
5. ConclusionIssue citation/warning; return documents; ensure safe re-entry.Clear roadway; monitor departure; complete documentation.

4.3 — Citations & Enforcement

Traffic citations. Use /ticket: subject name (ID), fine amount, violation reason. Issue before returning documents. Remain courteous; explain violation; provide court info.
Parking enforcement (MPS). Illegal parking: $1000 fine. Vehicles with $3000+ fines: mandatory impound. Contraband vehicles: immediate impound. Transport to Dillimore DMV.

4.4 — High-Risk / Felony Stops

Phase 1 — Positioning. Stop at 45° angle (nose out); do not exit initially; request 3–4 units Code 3; all units position for cover; exit with weapons drawn.

Phase 2 — Verbal commands (megaphone). "Driver and passengers, exit with hands visible" → "turn away, hands in the air" → "walk backward toward my voice" → "stop."

Phase 3 — Suspect control. Primary unit secures nearest suspect; secondary units secure others; maintain cover; proper handcuffing.

Phase 4 — Vehicle search. Systematic search after suspects secured; check all compartments; document evidence; arrange transport.

4.5 — Vehicle Pursuits

Initial broadcast: "10-80 on [color] [vehicle], occupied times [number]. Heading [direction] on [street] in [area]. Requesting assistance, Code 3."

Rules of engagement: Primary unit maintains pursuit and updates; secondary follows ready to take over; tertiary runs parallel/intercept; additional units stage at termination points. Maintain single-file — no overtaking without permission.

P.I.T. RequirementCriteria
Time elapsedMinimum 2 minutes of active pursuit
Units presentPrimary, secondary, tertiary in formation
AuthorizationPrimary calls "10-62" before attempting
ExecutionControlled contact to disable vehicle

Post-crash: disabled vehicle → verbal commands to exit; suspect flees on foot → Taser (all ranks) or Beanbag (Sergeant+ only); incapacitated → collect DNA, add charges; track to hospital if name known → arrest on discharge.

4.6 — Arrest & Detention

Transport requirements. Must use a 4-door vehicle (back seat). Two-door vehicles and motorcycles prohibited. Suspect must be cuffed and restrained; unrestrain only with Sergeant+ approval.
StepProcedure
1. IdentificationRequest name; transport to holding cells; fingerprint to verify; document false info.
2. SearchDon white search gloves; frisk for weapons/contraband; confiscate illegal items.
3. ProcessingComplete /arrest; enter badge number; set fine + jail time; no bail (enter 0).
4. CompletionSuspect transferred to custody by guards; radio "Resuming 10-8, 10-15 in custody"; complete paperwork.

Warning: setting bail will result in strike/suspension.

4.7 — Dispatch Priority

PriorityType & Response
Priority 1Immediate life threat — officer down, active shooter, serious injury accident, kidnapping. Code 3, all available units.
Priority 2Urgent, crime in progress — robbery/burglary, domestic with weapons, pursuit assist, recent felony. Code 2/3, multiple units.
Priority 3Routine — cold burglary/theft, non-injury accidents, noise complaints, parking. Code 1, single unit when available.

4.8 — Duty Status

RankOff-Duty Requirements
CadetMust remain on-duty at all times. Do not ask to go off-duty.
Officer / Officer IIRequest via radio with valid reason; Sergeant+ approval; max 30 minutes unless extended.
Sergeant+Request from another Sergeant+ (cannot self-approve); valid reason; higher rank may override.

Mandatory when going off-duty: return all department vehicles; store equipment in locker; change to civilian clothing; radio "Unit [#] 10-7"; return within 30 min. Prohibited off-duty: responding to calls, using department vehicles, wearing uniform, exercising police authority.

Failure to return on time results in disciplinary action.

Section 5 · Department Divisions

The LSPD operates through specialized divisions, each with unique operational standards, equipment authorization, and personnel requirements, working cohesively toward the department's mission.

5.1 — Field Training Operations (FTO)

"Excellence Through Training"

FTO is the backbone of recruitment and training — transforming civilian applicants into professional officers. Missions: application review & background checks, accept/deny recruitment, cadet training & mentorship, OIE administration, reinstated-officer retraining. Training covers basic law enforcement procedures, department policy, radio standards, vehicle certification, weapons qualification, and scenario exercises.

Selection: min. 2 weeks patrol experience, demonstrated teaching ability, knowledge of procedures, communication skills, supervisory approval.

5.2 — Patrol Division (PTL)

"The Thin Blue Line"

The largest and most visible component — the department's default division where all new officers begin. Missions: preventive high-visibility patrol, crime deterrence, emergency 911 response, initial crime investigation, traffic accident response, warrant service, crime scene preservation, community outreach.

Selection: completion of OIE exam, basic training graduation. Default assignment for new officers; foundation for specialized divisions.

5.3 — Special Weapons and Tactics (SWAT)

"Speed, Surprise, Violence of Action"

LSPD's elite tactical response unit for high-risk operations: barricaded suspects, hostage rescue, high-risk warrants, active shooter, gang suppression, narcotics raids, VIP protection, counter-terrorism. Vehicles: Enforcer, FBI Rancher, SWAT van, specialized pursuit units. Equipment: advanced tactical gear, breaching tools, less-lethal options, precision rifles.

Selection: exemplary record, superior fitness, advanced tactical training, psychological evaluation, leadership, quick decision-making. Activation: Code 10 pre-staging · Code 11 full deployment.

5.4 — Traffic Enforcement (TE)

"Pursuit to Apprehension"

Specializes in pursuit management, traffic enforcement, and street-racing intervention. Missions: lead high-speed pursuits, execute P.I.T., deploy spike strips/roadblocks, highway patrol, DUI interdiction, illegal racing suppression, vehicle impoundment. Fleet: Buffalo, Bullet, Turismo, Cheetah, Premier.

Selection: min. 4 weeks patrol experience, excellent driving record, fitness assessment, pursuit tactics certification, Sergeant+ recommendation.

5.5 — Internal Affairs (IA)

"Policing the Police"

Ensures accountability and integrity by investigating misconduct, policy violations, and ethical standards — operating independently for objectivity. Authority: excessive force complaints, corruption allegations, policy reviews, criminal conduct investigations, disciplinary recommendations, quality assurance audits. Reports directly to the Chief of Police with confidential complaint processing and whistleblower protection.

Selection: min. 2 weeks Patrol, investigative experience, impeccable integrity, advanced interview training, legal knowledge, command staff approval.

Inactive sub-divisions. Public Affairs (PA) and Human Resources (HR) are currently suspended pending reorganization. Their functions — public relations, press liaison, personnel file management, promotion documentation, department statistics — are temporarily handled by Command Staff.
